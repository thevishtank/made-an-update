    Hon. Suhaimi Gafar made an update about the Temburong Bridge

    Stephen StinnettStephen Stinnett December 3, 2019December 3, 2019 0 Comment

    H.R.H. Prince Haji Al-Muhtadee Billah and Chief Minister at the Prime Minister’s Office visited the Temburong Bridge Project sites in Kota Batu, Brunei-Muara and Labu Estate and Temburong. His delegation explored the single tower cable bridge that connects the northern and southern ramps of Jalan Kota Batu and the sub-water viaducts that cross Brunei.

    Southeast Asia’s soon to be the longest oversea bridge, Temburong Bridge is now 75 percent. It is almost complete and on track to meet its deadline in November 2019.

    The $1.6 billion projects is expected to be available to the public in 2020 after the finalization of the patchwork, which will take three to five months, according to Suhaimi Gafar, Brunei’s Minister of Development.

    The Minister also addressed the need for the authorities to ensure that contractors comply with all safety regulations and that they do not substantially increase work hours without approval.

    The bridge project began in 2009 with the launch of a proposal from the Ministry of Development to assess the feasibility of the bridge. The construction of a billion-dollar bridge started in 2014 after the government awarded contracts to Daelim, a South Korean firm, and China State Building Engineering Corporation.

    It is expected that the bridge will reduce travel time between the two districts by allowing commuters to bypass Limbang, Malaysia. It is also one of the largest public infrastructure projects in Brunei, connecting Brunei and Mura to Temburong.
